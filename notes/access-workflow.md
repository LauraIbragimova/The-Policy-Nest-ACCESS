# ACCESS Planning Framework

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** July 2026  
**Owner:** The Policy Nest

> This document defines the operational planning framework that powers the ACCESS Rollout Planner. It is grounded in publicly available CMS ACCESS Model guidance and structured for use as a data-backed planning template.

---

## Program Background

The CMS ACCESS (Advancing Chronic Care with Effective, Scalable Solutions) Model is a 10-year voluntary payment model launched December 2025. It introduces outcome-aligned payments (OAP) for Original Medicare beneficiaries receiving technology-enabled care for chronic conditions including hypertension, diabetes, chronic musculoskeletal pain, and depression. More than 150 organizations were accepted in the first cohort as of April 2026.

**Key program features:**
- 10-year voluntary model (2025–2035)
- Outcome-aligned payment (OAP) instead of activity-based requirements
- Technology-enabled care delivery required
- Condition tracks: hypertension, diabetes, MSK pain, depression
- Payer alignment pathway: major health plans have pledged adoption
- No PHI transmitted through the planner tool

## Rollout Phases

### Phase 1 — Readiness Assessment
**Duration:** 60–90 days before participation start  
**Goal:** Confirm eligibility, identify gaps, align leadership

| Task | Suggested Owner | Priority |
|------|----------------|----------|
| Review ACCESS eligibility criteria and condition track requirements | VBC Director / Strategy Lead | High |
| Confirm Medicare FFS patient population and qualifying conditions | Data/Analytics Team | High |
| Assess existing technology infrastructure against ACCESS requirements | IT/EHR Lead | High |
| Identify care team roles and staffing gaps | Operations Lead | High |
| Review outcome measurement framework and baseline performance | Quality/Clinical Lead | Medium |
| Conduct leadership alignment session | Executive Sponsor | High |
| Submit or confirm participation application | Compliance/Contracting | High |

**Key risks at this phase:**
- Eligibility misread (wrong condition track, wrong patient population)
- Leadership misalignment on tech investment requirements
- Underestimation of reporting infrastructure lead time

---

### Phase 2 — Operational Design
**Duration:** 60–90 days  
**Goal:** Design care workflows, assign team roles, select/confirm vendors

| Task | Suggested Owner | Priority |
|------|----------------|----------|
| Map current care delivery workflows against ACCESS model requirements | Clinical Operations Lead | High |
| Define care team roles (e.g., care coordinator, tech liaison, clinical lead) | HR / Operations | High |
| Evaluate and select technology vendor(s) for condition track | IT / VBC Director | High |
| Draft patient enrollment and alignment protocol | Care Coordination Team | High |
| Develop staff training plan | Training Lead | Medium |
| Build internal communication plan for ACCESS launch | Communications / Admin | Medium |
| Establish data governance and patient consent processes | Compliance | High |

**Key risks at this phase:**
- Vendor selection delays
- Workflow redesign resistance from clinical staff
- Unclear patient consent and data sharing protocols

---

### Phase 3 — Technology & Reporting Setup
**Duration:** 60–90 days  
**Goal:** Configure EHR, establish data feeds, stand up reporting infrastructure

| Task | Suggested Owner | Priority |
|------|----------------|----------|
| Configure EHR to capture ACCESS-required outcome data fields | IT / EHR Lead | High |
| Establish data feed from technology vendor to reporting systems | IT / Vendor | High |
| Build outcome tracking dashboard (internal) | Data/Analytics Team | High |
| Test data completeness and accuracy against ACCESS measure definitions | Quality Lead | High |
| Conduct end-to-end data flow validation | IT / Compliance | High |
| Document reporting workflow and assign ongoing ownership | Operations Lead | Medium |
| Complete any CMS-required technical onboarding steps | Compliance / IT | High |

**Key risks at this phase:**
- EHR configuration delays
- Data quality issues in outcome measures
- CMS reporting format mismatch

---

### Phase 4 — Enrollment & Launch
**Duration:** 30–60 days  
**Goal:** Align beneficiaries, train staff, go live

| Task | Suggested Owner | Priority |
|------|----------------|----------|
| Begin beneficiary voluntary alignment (prospective) | Care Coordination Team | High |
| Conduct staff training on ACCESS workflows and tools | Training Lead | High |
| Communicate launch timeline and expectations to clinical teams | Executive Sponsor | High |
| Activate technology vendor solution for enrolled patients | IT / Vendor | High |
| Confirm CMS go-live requirements are met | Compliance | High |
| Document baseline performance metrics for Year 1 comparison | Data/Analytics Team | Medium |

**Key risks at this phase:**
- Low beneficiary alignment rates
- Staff readiness gaps at go-live
- Technology activation delays

---

### Phase 5 — Monitoring & Optimization
**Duration:** Ongoing (Year 1+)  
**Goal:** Track performance, maintain compliance, improve outcomes

| Task | Suggested Owner | Priority |
|------|----------------|----------|
| Review outcome metrics monthly against OAP targets | Quality/Clinical Lead | High |
| Monitor CMS ACCESS program updates and guidance changes | VBC Director / Compliance | High |
| Conduct quarterly performance review with leadership | Executive Sponsor | High |
| Identify and address care gaps for aligned beneficiaries | Care Coordination Team | High |
| Update workflows and tools as needed based on performance data | Operations Lead | Medium |
| Track regulatory checkpoints and submission deadlines | Compliance | High |
| Prepare Year 1 performance summary | Data/Analytics Team | Medium |

**Key risks at this phase:**
- Outcome measure performance below OAP threshold
- Staff turnover affecting program continuity
- Missing CMS reporting deadlines

## Regulatory Checkpoints

| Checkpoint | Description | Phase |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Eligibility confirmation | Confirm organization and patient population meets ACCESS criteria | 1 |
| Participation application | Submit/confirm application with CMS | 1 |
| Vendor attestation | Confirm technology vendor meets ACCESS requirements | 2 |
| Data governance setup | Patient consent and data sharing protocols documented | 2 |
| EHR configuration validation | CMS-required data fields active and tested | 3 |
| CMS technical onboarding | Complete any required CMS system setup steps | 3 |
| Beneficiary alignment start | Confirm prospective alignment process is live | 4 |
| Go-live confirmation | CMS go-live requirements verified | 4 |
| Year 1 reporting | First outcome reporting cycle submitted | 5 |
| Annual performance review | OAP reconciliation and compliance review | 5 |

## Risk Register (Base)

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Eligibility misread | Medium | High | Review CMS FAQ and eligibility criteria with legal/compliance before applying |
| Vendor selection delay | High | High | Begin vendor evaluation in Phase 1 in parallel with readiness assessment |
| EHR configuration lag | High | High | Assign dedicated IT lead; build 30-day buffer into Phase 3 timeline |
| Low beneficiary alignment | Medium | High | Develop patient outreach strategy and staff scripts in Phase 2 |
| Staff resistance to workflow change | Medium | Medium | Early clinical champion identification; include frontline staff in design |
| CMS guidance change mid-rollout | Low | High | Monitor CMS ACCESS page and Federal Register weekly; maintain flexible workplan |
| Reporting data quality failure | Medium | High | Test data completeness in Phase 3 before go-live |
| Leadership misalignment | Low | High | Executive sponsor kickoff session in Phase 1 |
